Medical Management


Inspite of following dietary control, regular exercise and lifestyle modification, some patients are unable to retain their blood glucose level within normal limit. These patients require medicines.

Anti-Diabetic medicines are available in two forms:

  1. Oral (Tablets / Capsules):
    Most of the type 2 diabetes patients respond well to oral drugs. Metformin, Glipizide, Glimepiride, Pioglitazone, Voglibose, etc are mainly used oral anti-diabetic nowadays. They should be used strictly under medical guidance.

  2. Parentral (Injections):
    Insulin is the injectible anti-diabetic drug. Type-1 Diabetes patients are treated with insulin only. Type-2 diabets patients who do not respond well to oral drugs are treaated with insulin or a combination of Insulin and oral drugs.
    Types of Insulins available:
    1. Rapid Acting
    2. Short Acting
    3. Intermediate Acting
    4. Long Acting
    5. Mixed Insulin
    Again they should be used very strictly under medical guidance only.
